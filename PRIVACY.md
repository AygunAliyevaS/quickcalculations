Privacy Policy for MatrixAccel Pro

Effective Date: February 19, 2026
Last Updated: February 19, 2026

MatrixAccel Pro ("we", "us", "our") operates the MatrixAccel Pro service (the "Service"), a cloud-based SaaS platform providing an API for high-performance matrix computations. We are committed to protecting your privacy and handling your personal data responsibly. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, store, and protect information when you use our website (matrixaccel.pro or similar domain), API, dashboard, or any related services.

This Privacy Policy applies to personal data we collect as a data controller (e.g., your account information, billing details, usage metadata) and describes our practices as a data processor when handling content you submit via the API (e.g., matrices, computation inputs). For enterprise customers with a separate Data Processing Agreement (DPA), the DPA governs our processing of customer content on your behalf.

By using the Service, you agree to the practices described in this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree, please do not use the Service.

1. Information We Collect

We collect the following categories of information:

A. Information You Provide Directly

Account and contact information: name, email address, company name, phone number (if provided during sign-up or support).
Billing and payment information: processed securely via Stripe (we do not store full card details).
API keys and authentication credentials.
Support communications: messages, tickets, or feedback you send us.
Uploaded content / API inputs: matrices, numerical data, or files you submit for computation (treated as Customer Content below).

B. Information Collected Automatically

Usage data: API calls made, operations requested (e.g., multiply, transpose), matrix sizes processed, timestamps, success/failure status, GFLOPS consumed.
Technical and device information: IP address, browser type/version, operating system, device identifiers, referral URLs.
Log data: request metadata, error logs, performance metrics.
Cookies and similar technologies: session cookies for authentication, analytics cookies (e.g., via Microsoft Clarity or similar) for site usage insights.

C. Customer Content (Processed on Your Behalf)

Matrices, datasets, or other inputs you submit via the API. We act as a processor for this content and do not use it to train models, improve algorithms, or for any purpose beyond providing the Service, unless explicitly permitted under your account settings or agreement.
We do not collect sensitive personal data (e.g., racial/ethnic origin, health data, biometric data) and ask you not to submit such data via the API.

2. How We Use Your Information

We use the collected information for the following purposes:

To provide, maintain, and improve the Service (e.g., process API requests, display dashboard metrics).
To authenticate users, manage accounts, and generate API keys.
To process payments, subscriptions, and metered usage billing.
To communicate with you (e.g., account notifications, support responses, service updates).
To monitor, analyze, and prevent fraud, abuse, or security incidents.
To comply with legal obligations, resolve disputes, and enforce our Terms of Service.
To generate aggregated, anonymized statistics (e.g., average matrix size processed across users) for internal reporting and marketing (no re-identification possible).
For research and development: only on aggregated/anonymized data or with your explicit consent.

We do not use your API-submitted matrices or Customer Content to train AI models, sell data, or for advertising purposes.

3. Legal Basis for Processing (GDPR/EEA/UK/Switzerland)

For users in the EEA, UK, or Switzerland, our processing is based on:

Contract performance — fulfilling our service agreement (providing computations, managing your account).
Legitimate interests — improving security, preventing fraud, analytics (balanced against your rights).
Legal obligations — complying with tax, billing, or regulatory requirements.
Consent — where applicable (e.g., optional marketing communications; you can withdraw at any time).

4. Sharing and Disclosure of Information

We share information only as necessary:

With service providers and subprocessors (e.g., Azure for hosting/compute, Stripe for payments, email providers for notifications) under strict confidentiality and data protection agreements.
With Microsoft (Azure Marketplace) as required for transactable SaaS offers and billing integration.
To comply with legal requests, court orders, or regulatory authorities.
In connection with business transfers (e.g., merger, acquisition) — we will notify you if required.
To protect rights, property, or safety of MatrixAccel Pro, users, or the public.

We do not sell personal data or share it for cross-context behavioral advertising.

5. International Data Transfers

The Service is hosted on Microsoft Azure data centers (primarily in regions compliant with your preferences where possible). Data may be transferred to and processed in countries outside your residence (e.g., US, EU regions). We use appropriate safeguards such as Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs), adequacy decisions, or other GDPR-approved mechanisms for transfers.

6. Data Retention

We retain personal data only as long as necessary:

Account data: while your account is active + 30 days after closure (longer for legal/tax reasons).
Usage logs and billing records: up to 7 years for accounting and compliance.
API inputs (Customer Content): processed transiently during computation; stored temporarily only if required for debugging (with your consent) or as per your retention settings; generally deleted immediately after response delivery.
Aggregated analytics: indefinitely in de-identified form.

You may request deletion of your account and associated data (subject to legal retention obligations).

7. Security

We implement commercially reasonable technical, administrative, and organizational measures to protect your data from unauthorized access, loss, misuse, or alteration, including encryption in transit (HTTPS/TLS), access controls, regular security assessments, and monitoring. However, no system is completely secure; please protect your API keys and credentials.
